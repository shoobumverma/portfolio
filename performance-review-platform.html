360 Degree Performance Review - Redesign Case study Redesign of the 360 degree performance review under the performance management module of sumHR
Company sumHR Date shipped March 2021 Platform Web
Preface
Introduction to the platform

sumHR digitizes and automates HR processes to reduce paperwork, increase employee engagement, and guide HR teams from data to decision. 360 Degree Performance Review is part of the performance management solution that sumHR provides.

360 Degree reviews allow for periodic multilevel feedback to employees; promoting development and growth in the workplace. These reviews unfold crucial data points that help both the employee and the company in collaboration, proving to be an essential tool in the HR toolkit.

Problem
The problem

The module was actively supporting 30+ companies and their XX+ employees, but HR teams were in constant need of assistance from our customer success (CS) team. Also, the setup process in particular was fully handled by a CS representative even though the platform was built to be self configurable. The last of the complaints were of employees unable to find review forms and hence often missing deadlines.

This was an undesirable situation as the platform alone should be able to support the performance review needs and guide the HR managers in making decisions.

Project Intent
Enable HR teams to independently run and manage 360 degree performance reviews for their employees, on time.
Context
My role

I led the design process and worked mostly independently on the project. But regular interaction with a co-designer, manager and engineers allowed for a collaborative process.

The skills displayed in this role were UX Design, research, and writing.

Context
Users & audience

sumHR is used in office work environments across a variety of industries. The platform can be divided into three different areas that serve different personas based on their role in the company and HR team.

Problem
Scope and constraints

Time constraints did not allow for an adequate number of user interviews to be conducted, neither was I able to interview all the different personas.

Lack of time allotted for development also resulted in certain experiences to be left unaltered.
